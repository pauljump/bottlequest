### 5 New Directions Proposed (Round 2)

1. **"Anti-Hype Social Network"** - Post bottles you drank with brutally honest takes. Upvotes only if you tried both the hyped version AND the better cheap alternative. Status comes from debunking expensive bottles, not collecting them. Tinder-style curation for approving "actually tried it" receipts.

2. **"Bottle Bingo with Stakes"** - Weekly game: predict which $100 bottle will beat which $1K+ bottle in blind taste-off. Players stake predictions, winners split pot. AI analyzes tasting notes from experts to generate weekly matchups. Your friend curates the matchups, other friend runs the odds/payouts math.

3. **"Expert Clone Chatbot Marketplace"** - You recruit Nick Johnson (and 20 others like him). They do 1hr recorded tasting session. AI trains a chatbot clone of them. Users text "Is Pappy 23 worth $2.5k?" and get Nick's actual take. Experts get paid per query. AI infra = voice cloning + taste preference modeling.

4. **"Hype Index Futures"** - Track "hype score" vs "actual quality score" for every bottle. Let users bet on which bottles are overvalued. When hype crashes (like Pappy), betters win. Turn the information asymmetry into a prediction market. SQL friend runs the market engine, curator friend labels quality.

5. **"Taste Cluster Matchmaking"** - Not bottle recommendations - PEOPLE recommendations. "You and @whiskeynerd47 have 94% taste overlap. He just found something you'll love." Make the flex finding your taste twin, not finding rare bottles. AI infra = collaborative filtering on taste preferences at ingredient level (peated, wheated, etc).

### User Interest in #4 (Hype Index Futures)
**User:** "4 sounds intereting. is this different than polymarkets?"

**Response:** Explained similarities and differences:
- **Same:** Prediction market mechanism, users stake positions, market resolves on outcome
- **Different:** Domain-specific (bottles), fuzzy resolution (not binary yes/no), continuous scores
- **Problem:** Resolution mechanism unclear - who decides if bottle is overvalued? Expert consensus? Blind tests? Messy.

**Alternative angles proposed:**
- "Taste prediction game" - predict which bottle YOU will prefer
- "Debunk bounties" - blind taste-offs with video proof, collect bounty if you prove expensive bottle isn't worth it
